Beyond the Style Guide

Despite having a style guide and front-end framework in place, Lone Star's team consistently pushed me to stretch the boundaries with my designs. This encouragement led to a number of experimental concepts, such as this Google Map view with an overlay. Thanks to constructive and actionable feedback from the team, we finalized and shipped each page after a handful of iterations.

About Us, Infographic Style

Speaking of pushing design boundaries, the Lone Star team had the idea of replacing the standard About Us page with an infographic. I hadn't done an infographic at this scale before, but thought it'd be something fun to learn and quickly jumped into the unknown.

After organizing a slew of content in Google Docs, the Lone Star team and I worked on a design that would tell the Lone Star story top to bottom. At almost 4000 pixels high, the resulting page combines history, data, and a bit of humor with a range of visual aids.
