Responsive Emails

I started coding responsive emails in early 2013, but after creating a few templates I noticed that I'd reinvented the wheel with each one. So I created a master template that contains a few layout ideas for HTML emails that adapt to small screens and respond to breakpoints.

It also includes workarounds for a few HTML email gotchas in such as dodgy box model support in Hotmail, auto-detected links in Yahoo!, and background images in Outlook. With most of my best-practices in this one template, I started most of my emails using the template, ripping out what I didn't need and customizing the rest.

This was too awesome to keep to myself.

Support Details

When Canfield's tech support team starts helping a client, the conversation usually starts with “What is your OS / browser / network settings / etc.”? Seeing an opportunity to work on something fun and off-the-books, I designed a concept for an app that grabs the technical details of a computer environment. From this, a co-worker and I built a working prototype on our spare time.

Written in JavaScript, the app is quite small and portable. It's already been forked into two other projects, proving to be quite useful to our support team.
