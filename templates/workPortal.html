* Most of the app is confidential and contains sensitive types of data, so I'll focus on the process of designing and building it rather than what the app actually does.
My Role
  • Product Design
  • HTML / CSS / RWD

Planning

Bring 3D photography to the web by translating our existing desktop software into user-friendly, web based suite of tools.

Research, evolution, wireframes, and such.

Existing products and doctor practices gave the interface a myriad of edge cases to accommodate. Plus small devices.

Patient Side

Our focus was clear from the start: patient images are priority #1, everything else comes second. I created a dark style guide to highlight the photos and a small bit of interface and data visualization. Early in the process, I experimented with the contrast, spacing, ad overall size of the interface in an effort to strike the right balance of “useful but not distracting”.

We spent a lot of time designing the sign-up / log-in flow with a focus on security. Data protection and HIPAA compliance were paramount at every stage, given the sensitive nature of the app's content.

The desktop software we modeled the app after is rather complex. I frequently fought for simplicity and made cases for leaving out a number of extra “would be nice” features. I won some and lost some, but the app is better for it.

Practice Side

We provide doctors with several tools to monitor website activity and close deals with prospective patients. Doctors can pull up a master list of prospective patients and immediately see who is most interested based on their activity. From there, each practice can dig into a patient's activity, see their photos, and even message them directly through the web.
